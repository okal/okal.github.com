1. Recognition rather than recall

Don't make me think!

- Random website visitor

Human beings have limited working memory. Let your users spend theirs on the task at hand, not learning how to use your website or application.

Users shouldn't have to remember how to carry out an action using your interface. It should be obvious from the controls.

2. Aesthetic and Minimalistic Design

Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but nothing left to take away.

- Antoine de Saint Exupery

2. Aesthetic and Minimalistic Design

Present only the relevant information for a given context. Anything extra competes for the user's attention, to the detriment of the experience.

7. Help & Documentation

For some applications, it may still be necessary to provide comprehensive documentation in spite of the designer's effort.

Make it easy to search, clear - providing steps if possible, concise and contextual.

8. User Control & Freedom

It's OK to make mistakes.

Give user's the freedom to explore your interface without fear.

Pro Tip: "Undo/Redo" is your friend.

9. Consistency and Standards

9. Consistency and Standards

It can be tempting to go out of your way to reinterpret common UI elements.

DON'T!

Well, not all the time.

Users expect things to work a certain way. It is often a better choice to use native OS widgets to enhance usability. Overly styled form elements, for example, may confuse a user.

10. Flexibility & Efficiency of Use
